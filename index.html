About Me

I've always been a data collector — long before I knew the term "data analyst."

One of my earliest memories is of spending an afternoon watching anthills and carefully cataloging the ants’ behavior. That curiosity about patterns and systems has followed me ever since. I’ve always found myself collecting information about the things around me and looking for relationships hidden within.

Professionally, I spent over six years working as a behaviorist, where collecting and analyzing behavioral data was a central part of my work. Today I bring that same analytical mindset to data analysis, using tools like Python, SQL, and visualization platforms to explore datasets, clean and transform data, and uncover meaningful insights.

For me, working with data isn’t something new — it’s a way of thinking that has been present throughout my life.
